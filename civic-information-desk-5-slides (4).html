02 · The solution Civic Information Desk

One front desk. Four clerks. Same model.

A single chat surface routes each question to the right departmental persona and answers it with a live language model. The persona is a prompt, not a separate model — one endpoint, four voices.

Persona 01

Permits

Brisk and efficient. No wasted words — the answer comes first.

Persona 02

Tax

Patient and reassuring. Flags how to avoid a penalty before it lands.

Persona 03

Regulations

Dry, a little wry — precise, and never sarcastic toward the resident.

Persona 04

General

Warm front-desk voice. Its job is helping you find the right window.

AI chat assistant — routed to the department that owns the question Voice assistant — a streaming call with the same clerk Document guidance — the model reasons over the file you attach Multilingual support — English, Spanish, Hindi Appointment booking — the model reads the thread and drafts the form Nearby office finder — the handoff when you want a person Scenario check — a chat mode: verdict, why, and what to settle first

Every persona is BASE_RULES plus one short paragraph of voice: 2–4 sentence answers, plain redirects when a question is out of scope, and never an invented fee, date, or phone number.

03 · Architecture Civic Information Desk

Four layers, one model call.

  "language", "context" }

Four routing fields around one question field — which is why adding a feature rarely means adding a code path.

Why a gateway

The app talks to one OpenAI-compatible endpoint, with BASE_URL, MODEL, and API_KEY read from .env and failing fast at startup if missing. Changing the model — or the provider — is a config edit, not a code change.
